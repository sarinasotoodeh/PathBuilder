Overview

An apprenticeship is a structured pathway combining paid work experience with formal education. You work for an employer in a skilled trade, earning wages while learning from experienced professionals. This "learn while you earn" model is popular in trades, construction, electrical work, plumbing, and other hands-on fields.

Learning Style

Apprenticeships combine practical and theoretical learning:

  • On-the-job training with experienced mentors
  • Classroom instruction in related theory
  • Hands-on projects and real-world applications
  • Safety certifications and trade-specific skills
  • Formal exams to progress through levels

Who It's Best For

  • Students who love hands-on, practical work
  • Those interested in skilled trades
  • People who prefer learning on the job
  • Those seeking stable, well-paying careers
  • Students looking to minimize education costs
